SOYBEAN is expanding across the Cerrado

SOYBEAN

Soybean cultivation is expanding significantly in the Brazilian Cerrado, transforming pastures and areas of native vegetation into cropland, with growth exceeding 1400% between 1985 and 2021, occupying approximately 10% of the biome. This generates environmental and social concerns, especially in regions like Matopiba, despite sustainability efforts and monitoring of expansion in consolidated areas.
The area planted with soybeans in the Cerrado increased by 49% between 2013/14 and 2022/23, reaching 23.15 million hectares (50.1% of the national soybean production).
The expansion occurs both through the conversion of pastures (as in the Pampa, which also suffers from soybean cultivation) and through the direct transformation of native vegetation, according to MapBiomas. The advance of soybean cultivation is one of the main drivers of deforestation and degradation of the Cerrado, a biome rich in biodiversity, with impacts on rainfall patterns and water resources, warns the agency.
